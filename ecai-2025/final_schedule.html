ECAI-2025 — Schedule

This is the schedule for the technical sessions at ECAI-2025, intended specifically for the authors of the papers to be presented during these sessions. A public schedule with precise timings and full information on other programme items, such as keynote talks and further panel sessions, will become available at the ECAI website in good time.

Every contributed paper marked with a T will get presented by means of a full talk during a 15-minute slot (12-minute talk + questions). Every contributed paper paper marked with a P will get presented by means of a 2-minute spotlight talk during the session indicated, followed by a poster presentation during a 90-minute poster session in the late afternoon of the same day. The same is true for papers marked with a RP; these are the papers for which authors specifically requested a poster slot. Each 'Frontiers in AI' talk will get a 30-minute slot (25-minute talk + questions).

We consider it very important that every paper gets presented in person, but also understand that some authors will face difficulties attending the conference. For papers to be presented by a proxy, the name of that person is also shown in the schedule. In case of unexpected difficulties in attending, please do your best to find a proxy and let us know by email right away. There is no option to present remotely or to replay a pre-recorded presentation on site. We reserve the right to mark papers as no-shows on the conference website in case they end up not being presented or in case proxy arrangements are not adequately communicated to us.

In case of questions, you can reach us at program-chairs@ecai2025.org. This schedule respects all author requests regarding scheduling constraints that reached us in good time. We won't be able to consider any further such requests. We believe this schedule to be final, but cannot fully exclude the need for future updates. Should we need to make a change after 22 September 2025 that affects you directly, we will let you know by email.

Curious how we built the schedule? You can read all about it in this technical report.
